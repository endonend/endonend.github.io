<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469259">I feel blessed to be entering my 6th year of fatherhood.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469265">The last five years have been challenging and very tiring. Beyond having a five year old and twin (almost) four year olds, balancing home life, work, marriage, and other relationships is exhausting. I wouldn't trade it for the world, though.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469268">I've grown more in the last five years than probably any other period in my life. Constantly learning, growing, and adjusting to become a better person, a better parent, and a better husband. I know it's worth it because I have three little girls that absolutely love and  adore me. Not to mention a happy wife who loves and appreciates me -- someone who brings a smile to my face every time I see her walk into a room. I couldn't ask for anything more.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469271">So what's the secret? Leo Babauta says it a lot better than I could in his post <a href="http://zenhabits.net/great-dad/">The Tiny Guide to Being a Great Dad</a>. Three simple rules, really. His post could easily be re-named <em>The Tiny Guide to Being a Great Person</em>. It is universal.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469280"><strong>Be There. Love Them. Be Present.</strong></p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469284">Everything beyond that is icing on the cake.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469287">The hardest part is not getting caught up in the stress and hectic nature of our life. With three kids virtually the same age, full time jobs, side jobs to make extra money, managing a household, making time for friends, and (most importantly -- I learned this the hard way) doing everything possible to constantly improve your relationship with your spouse... this can be a challenge. It's tiring and ridiculously hard work. It's easy to get caught up, even when you think you have things under control. Believe me, I know.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469290">The key thing, for me, is living in the moment as much as I can, with a dash of patience, purpose, perseverance, humility, and laughter.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469293">It means making time for your kids when they ask. Even if it's something you've done a hundred times before.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469296">It means being present and focused on your wife when you finally get a chance to have a date or spend just a few minutes together.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469299">It's being patient, calm, and thoughtful when your life feels exactly the opposite.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469302">It's cherishing those memories you get an opportunity to re-experience with your kids. (But not re-living your life through your children. It's a fine balance.)</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469305">It's giving your kids the skills to solve problems, be independent, and make good decisions. Then getting the hell out of the way. (Thanks <a href="http://www.merlinmann.com/">Merlin Mann</a> for that great tip.)</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469311">It's giving up on any sense of manliness or who you think society thinks you should be -- those darn <a href="https://jason-dettbarn-ecz8.squarespace.com/end-on-end/2012/03/expectations">expectations</a>. Just be real. Let your guard down. Dance, be silly, and do crazy things like slide head first down your kid's princess slip and slide with all your clothes on. Show your kids it's OK to be weird, to be yourself, and to be creative.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469317">It's forgiving yourself and those you love when mistakes are made. Then working your ass off to make things right (and better.)</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_469320">It's tearing up to a Taylor Swift song at your daughter's dance recital. (Don't judge.)</p>
