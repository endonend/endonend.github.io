<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_657544">When it's either incredibly exciting or frustrating to be interested in Apple and tech: iPhone rumor/leak time.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_657547">Yes, it technically started earlier in the year (or more likely never stopped in the first place), but the weeks leading up to a new iPhone launch are always the worst.</p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_657550">Case in point, the 9to5Mac RSS feed, which just posted two contradicting stories within the span of two minutes.</p>

<img src="http://endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/nope-yep.png" title="" alt=""/>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_657556">Sometimes I long for a slow web, when there wasn't a rush to break news or post a hundred "stories" a day, strictly for pageviews. </p>

<p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1407674114902_657559">Any other RSS/news/info addicts out there? How do you balance that interest with the overwhelming amount of over-publishing on the web today?</p>
