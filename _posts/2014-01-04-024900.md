<h4 id="lovelist">Love List</h4>

<em>noun</em>

<ol>
    <li>A fluid list of things you want to start or spend more time doing in the new year. For past examples, see <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/love-list-2013/">2013</a> and <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/my-2012-love-list/">2012</a>.</li>
</ol>

<hr />

Prior to looking back at <a href="http://endonend.org/posts/love-list-2013/">last year's list</a>, I thought for sure I didn't do well. But I did -- mainly due to picking things I was already committed to because they were important to me.

Some things I didn't quite get to, like the master bedroom makeover (we did buy a new bed frame and do some serious reorganization, so it is much nicer at least.) I also didn't eat vegetarian for an entire month, but we did eat vegetarian meals <strong>much</strong> more often. And finally the kids book is something I still want to work on, but it's also something that is going to be insanely time consuming. Someday.

I did pretty darn well on the rest. I'll write up a recap of 2013 soon to cover more of the year in detail.

Anyway, since we're already past New Year's Day, here's my Love List for 2014:

<ol>
    <li><strong>Continue to be on top of my priorities &amp; make sure I fund them.</strong> No procrastination.</li>
    <li><strong>Launch three <a href="http://ekowave.com">Eko Wave</a> sites</strong>. Two may be coming fairly soon, I hope.</li>
    <li><strong>Write more</strong>. This will involve journaling in <a href="http://dayoneapp.com/">Day One</a> and blogging, which is one of the reasons I just switched to using <a href="http://anchorcms.com">Anchor</a> for this blog. It's so easy to add posts.)</li>
    <li><strong>Read more</strong>, but less noise. I need to re-visit my RSS and content consumption strategy to figure out where I need to be to get the most value.</li>
    <li><strong>Exercise more</strong>. On everyone's new year list ever, I suppose.</li>
    <li><strong>Drink more water</strong>. I am starting out with a daily goal of 64 ounces and working my way up to 100. I've been tracking my intake for a month and you'd be surprised how little water I drank in the past.</li>
    <li><strong>Keep purging "stuff"</strong>. Less is more!</li>
    <li><strong>Eat less</strong>. Portions are my down fall.</li>
</ol>

Happy New Year everyone. Please feel free to share your resolutions, love list, etc. in the comments.
