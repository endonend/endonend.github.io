Over the weekend, I finally got around to a project I’ve been wanting to do: repurpose a tree round from the tree they cut down in front of our house earlier this spring. Plus, the round was finally dried out enough that I could 1.) move it and 2.) get the bark off in a reasonable amount of time.

The best part was Lucy and Stella (two of my three daughters) were super excited to help out!

Here are the basic steps:

<ol>
<li><strong>De-bark the tree round</strong>. I used a hammer and pry bar. Most of it came off pretty easy, although there were a few stubborn areas.
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step1-300x225.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="225" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4925" /></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Sand it down</strong>. I used a combination of fine and medium sandpaper blocks to get the round nice and smooth to the touch. Stella and Lucy were <strong>huge</strong> helps in sanding this behemoth.
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step2-225x300.jpeg" alt="" width="225" height="300" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4924" /></p></li>
<li><p>Once you are done sanding, <strong>wipe the round down with a wet, lint-free towel</strong> to prep it for staining.
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step3-300x225.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="225" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4923" /></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Put the legs on</strong>. This was actually sort of difficult, as the round was pretty uneven on the bottom. I did end up finding four spots that were equidistant apart and fairly centered on the bottom for support. I used Ikea Capita legs that are adjustable, so we can level the table on any surface.
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step4-225x300.jpeg" alt="" width="225" height="300" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4922" />
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step4b-300x225.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="225" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4921" /></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Stain it</strong>. I used a honey maple stain, which ended up darker than I pictured, but looks good nonetheless. Stella and Lucy helped stain the top, as well.
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step5-300x225.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="225" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4920" /></p></li>
<li><p><strong>Finish with a polyurethane</strong>. I used a fast drying clear satin polyurethane to protect the wood and give it a nice, rustic look:
<img src="http://www.endonend.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/step6-300x225.jpeg" alt="" width="300" height="225" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4919" /></p></li>
</ol>

<p>I did three coats on the sides and six plus on the top, since it soaks in so&nbsp;much.

So pleased with how it turned out, but most of all I was glad to make something with my hands <strong>and</strong> have Lucy and Stella help out. A great experience for all of us!
