Each of the <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/my-love-list-for-2014/">last</a> <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/love-list-2013/">few</a> <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/my-2012-love-list/">years</a> I’ve spent some time documenting and tracking my love list.

<blockquote>
  <b>Love List</b>: A fluid list of things you want to start or spend more time doing in the new year.
</blockquote>

The process begins with a review at the end of the year, as I look back at what I accomplished, learned, and want to improve on in the coming year.
<!--more-->

At the start of 2014, I wrote down 8 things:

<ol>
    <li><i><strong>Continue to be on top of my priorities &amp; make sure I fund them.</strong> No procrastination.</i>

<b>Review</b>: Overall I feel this was a success. The biggest area of focus for this item was my marriage. The Mrs. and I put a serious amount of work into our relationship over the last couple years, to reconnect and build a better relationship. I feel like that work paid off this year.

Other aspects include the kids, work, and personal projects. I don’t feel as successful in these areas, even though there was much success. The kids are certainly growing up fast, turning into three smart, confident young ladies. I completed my first full year in my new career as a UX professional and made great improvements during that time. I launched my first built-from-scratch <a href="http://relicscout.com">Ruby on Rails project</a> through <a href="http://ekowave.com">my side business</a>, as well as helped my good friend <a href="http://buffalosmallpress.org">re-launch a website</a> and kickstart a <a href="http://itinerantprinter.com">new project</a>. I certainly got a lot done, even if deep down I know I can do better. I really need to figure out what that means for 2015.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Launch three <a href="http://ekowave.com/">Eko Wave</a> sites</strong>. Two may be coming fairly soon, I hope.</i>

<b>Review</b>: We completed one, which is going through a process of re-imagining, while another is slowly moving toward launch. We certainly have a ton of ideas. Our biggest problem is figuring out which opportunity is the right one to focus on. Certainly an area of improvement in 2015.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Write more</strong>. This will involve journaling in <a href="http://dayoneapp.com/">Day One</a> and blogging, which is one of the reasons I just switched to using <a href="http://anchorcms.com/">Anchor</a> for this blog. It’s so easy to add posts.)</i>

<b>Review</b>: I am still writing here (32 posts in 2014), with a couple of diversions and tinkering (I moved from Anchor to Squarespace and back to WordPress during 2014!) I want to write more, so this will be a focus in 2015 as well.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Read more</strong>, but less noise. I need to re-visit my RSS and content consumption strategy to figure out where I need to be to get the most value.</i>

<b>Review</b>: I read a ton of comic books and graphic novels in 2014. Not much in the way of books, but I do have a few in progress, as well as a lengthy queue. My RSS reader is constantly being tuned and I feel pretty good about that now. I’ve cut back on Twitter and Facebook usage, which has really helped with the noise.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Exercise more</strong>. On everyone’s new year list ever, I suppose.</i>

<b>Review</b>: I’ve made an effort to move more and since moving to a standing desk I rarely sit down during the work week. Serious exercise is few and far between, however. One positive achievement is walking our brand new (in 2014) dog: I take him on daily walks during the work week. That has been a big help.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Drink more water</strong>. I am starting out with a daily goal of 64 ounces and working my way up to 100. I’ve been tracking my intake for a month and you’d be surprised how little water I drank in the past.</i>

<b>Review</b>: I’ve been tracking this the entire year. I  did well until recently. I was in the 64 to 100 ounce range every day, until the last few months. I’ve been working to get back in habit — I have no idea what caused me to break the habit though!</li>
    <li><i><strong>Keep purging “stuff”</strong>. Less is more!</i>

<b>Review</b>: we continue to make strides through garage and consignment sales. I plan to keep this on my list for 2015 as well. It’s a never-ending challenge.</li>
    <li><i><strong>Eat less</strong>. Portions are my down fall.</i>

<b>Review</b>: overall I’ve made improvements, but I still feel like I go overboard a little too often. Between this item, the standing desk, and daily dog walks I’ve lost like 10 pounds since early 2014. A success, but I also know I can do better with more consistent, serious exercise.</li>
</ol>

Overall, I am pretty happy with how 2014 turned out. Things happened a little slower than I planned, but in the end I made positive steps in all the areas I wanted to focus on in 2014. Looking forward to 2015, I want to focus on the improvement areas I mentioned above, as well as some new ideas and goals I’m considering. I’ll leave those details for my 2015 Love List post. Stay tuned!
