Taking a <a href="http://zenhabits.net/parent/">cue from Leo Babauta</a> over at Zen Habits:

<blockquote>
  It’s nearly Father’s Day, and that always causes me to reflect on what kind of father I am. I think I’m pretty decent, though I’m not where I’d like to be.
</blockquote>

Leo's list is pretty great. For me, I aspire to:

<ol>
<li>Be slower to anger and frustration.</li>
<li>Be less critical of mistakes. </li>
<li>Be less concerned about their future and more concerned with the present moment.</li>
<li>Inspire them to be passionate about the things they like, but have less expectations of the path they take to find and use those passions.</li>
<li>Inspire them to be honest, courageous, empathetic, and vulnerable to experience life and relationships in the best way possible.</li>
</ol>

I have a lot to learn about each of these, but I also have four (including my wife) great teachers to help me along the way.
