<blockquote>People realized when the numbers were gone, they had been using them to decide whether to like something. I certainly didn't expect these tendencies of people saying, 'I literally don't know what to do [without knowing the metrics].'</blockquote>

Ben Grosser created a browser extension (used by 1000s of people) that removes the numbers from Facebook like/friend/share counts and also recently <a href="http://computationalculture.net/article/what-do-metrics-want-how-quantification-prescribes-social-interaction-on-facebook">published a paper</a> examining the impact of metrics in the journal <em>Computational Culture</em>.

<em>via <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2014/11/how-numbers-on-facebook-change-our-behavior/382005/?single_page=true">How Numbers on Facebook Change Behavior - The Atlantic</a>.</em>
