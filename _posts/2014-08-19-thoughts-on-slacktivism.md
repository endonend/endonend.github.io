From Seth Godin's <a href="http://sethgodin.typepad.com/seths_blog/2014/08/slacktivism.html">blog post on slacktivism</a>:

<blockquote>According to recent <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/08/18/business/ice-bucket-challenge-has-raised-millions-for-als-association.html?_r=0">data</a> about the ice bucket challenge making the rounds, more than 90% of the people mentioning it (posting themselves being doused or passing on the word) didn't make a donation to support actual research on an actual disease. Sounds sad, no?</blockquote>

Truly sad. I get raising awareness and that a lot of money <em>has</em> been donated, but for a vast majority of people it seems like a classic case of slacktivism. I think it's especially valid when awareness reaches a critical mass. Once that point has been hit, additional "awareness" has less of an impact if donations are not increasing at the same rate as the meme.

My wife and I were challenged and didn't respond with a video or immediate donation, so with that said we will be donating today to the following organizations:

<ul>
    <li><a href="https://my.charitywater.org/down-under">charity:water</a>: If, based on the estimates from the NY Times article above, a few million people did the Ice Bucket Challenge, that's a million plus gallons of water wasted. With the drought in the western U.S. and the fact that over a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_scarcity">billion people have no access to clean drinking water</a>, that seems kind of dumb.</li>
    <li><a href="http://www.nationalmssociety.org/">MS Society</a>: for my sister and the two million plus people that have the disease globally.</li>
    <li><a href="http://www.lupus.org/">Lupus Foundation of America</a>: for my mom, grandfather, and a million plus people that have the disease globally.</li>
    <li><a href="http://www.alsa.org">ALS Association</a>: for inspiring a lot of people to make donations to a cause.</li>
</ul>

Will you join me in making a donation to a cause that means something to you? No video necessary.
