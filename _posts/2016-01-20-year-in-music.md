As I <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/2014-music/">mentioned last year</a>, I was leaning toward moving back to Beats Music. That came true in 2015 with the launch of Apple Music, the replacement for Beats Music. The service got off to a rough start, but incremental improvements have been released through out 2015 and I now have very few complaints.
<!--more-->

Here are my favorite releases for 2015:

<strong>1. Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly</strong>
(One of the best hip hop albums in the last 5 or 10 years, if not ever. Hi Chris!)
<strong>2. Tame Impala - Currents</strong>
(Their strongest, most cohesive album yet.)
<strong>3. Ryan Adams - 1989</strong>
(Adams takes Taylor Swift's songs in a new, melancholy direction and it's amazing.)
<strong>4. Sleater-Kinney - No Cities to Love
</strong>(One of the best live bands I've ever seen, returns from an almost 10 year break with one of their best.)
<strong>5. Foals - What Went Down</strong>
(What Went Down combines the best of the dance-y post-punk with straight forward alt-rock to produce a great album.)
<strong>6. Discourse - Sanity Decays</strong>
(One of the better live hardcore bands I've seen in a while and a pretty darn good album.)
<strong>7. Courtney Barnett - Sometimes I Sit and Think, Sometimes I Just Sit</strong>
(Kind of like an Australian Liz Phair. So good.)
<strong>8. Chvrches - Every Open Eye</strong>
(I am a sucker for this kind of indie-influenced pop music. Chvrches delivers every time.)
<strong>9. Beauty Pill - Beauty Pill Describes Things As They Are</strong>
(Beauty Pill returns from a long hiatus to deliver an amazing, eclectic take on 2015.)
<strong>10. Saintseneca - Such Things</strong>
(Not as mind blowing as Dark Arc, but that is asking a lot.)
