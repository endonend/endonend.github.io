Nick Wynja's <a href="http://hackmake.org/2014/08/paradox-and-perspective">post on Hack/Make</a> hits close to home:

<blockquote>Having a trusted system means you gain the mental capacity to take on more but do we always have the human capacity to take on more?</blockquote>

Productivity. Doing more. <em>Always more</em>. It's something I've been wrestling with lately and I know a few others in the same boat. But here's the rub: there is <strong>always</strong> more to do if you are looking.

And this is where focusing on productivity fails:

<blockquote>But the most rewarding stuff in life doesn’t manifest itself on a list, so our focus on organizing life is futile.</blockquote>

So true.

The hardest thing is figuring out what you <em>need</em> to do... need, meaning the things that will keep you (and the people you are responsible for) happy and healthy. Many of those decisions involve living outside your <a href="http://www.endonend.org/posts/how-to-find-your-life-purpose-an-unconventional-approach">personal bubble</a>.
